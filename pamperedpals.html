Pampered Pals Case Study: Designing an online store

My Role

solo student project for Career Foundry UI/UX bootcamp.

Objective

I designed a responsive e-commerce website catering to the pet supplies sector which is the second largest consumer product group (CPG) in e- commerce and continues to outpace brick and mortar stores.

Problem

Problem: There is a lot of confusion about natural wine and no government issued label to identify natural wines. Natural wine is complicated but in general terms it is farmed organically (biodynamically) and made without adding or removing anything during the winemaking process.

Pampered Pals targets shoppers looking for high quality dog and cat products. It congregates everything in one place for users who don’t have time to make a trip to physical stores.

User Flows

I picked 2 user stories to fulfill in this E-commerce app and created a user flow outlining all the steps involved.

1) As a new customer, I want to access the inventory without having to register, so that I can make sure this store has what I’m looking for before having to create an account.

2) As a customer, I want there to be a variety of payment options, so that I can choose the payment method that suits me best.
