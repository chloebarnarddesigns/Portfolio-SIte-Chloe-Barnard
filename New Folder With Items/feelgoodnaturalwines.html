Feel Good Natural Wines Case Study: Designing a Native IOS and Android App

My Role

solo student project for Career Foundry UI/UX bootcamp.

Objective

I designed an app that connects users to a marketplace for only natural wines. Users can order directly from the application and also scan wine labels to determine if they are natural wine or regular wine.

Problem

Problem: There is a lot of confusion about natural wine and no government issued label to identify natural wines.

Natural wine is complicated but in general terms it is farmed organically (biodynamically) and made without adding or removing anything during the winemaking process.

Solution

Feel Good Natural Wines does all the hard work and research into winemakers for you and guarantees a supply of only natural wines.

Users can browse and purchase natural wines and also scan a wine bottle to determine if it is a natural wine match.

Research

I compared and analyzed existing applications in the wine and beverage space to better understand the users’ needs when browsing wine options, purchasing wines, and scanning wine labels. I made a note of helpful UI patterns used in the current market.

Customer Journey

I mapped out all the steps needed to complete 2 core user flows for the application.

1) I want to be able to search and purchase natural wine

2) I want to be able to scan a bottle of wine and determine if it is considered natural wine or not

IOS Human Interface vs. Google Material Design

I compared the human interface guidelines for iOS devices to Google’s Material Design guidelines for Android devices. After mapping out the steps needed to complete the user flows, I began analyzing the design guidelines for iOS vs Android native applications.

After analyzing the key features for both device types, I designed one set of wireframes for iOS devices and designed another set of wireframes for Android devices. Below you can see a side by side comparison of the payment details screen for both versions.
