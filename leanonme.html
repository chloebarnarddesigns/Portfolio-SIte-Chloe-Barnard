Lean on Me

A Location Recommendation Application that Connects Users to Covid-19 Aid or Donation/Volunteer Opportunities

Project Background

My Role

Intensive solo student project and end-to-end case study for Career Foundry UI/UX bootcamp with the guidance of my tutor and mentor.

Timeline

Feb.18, 2021 - April 10, 2021 , April 29, 2021 - June 01, 2021 | (11 weeks)

Tools

Objective

The challenge was to design a location recommendation application with responsive screen designs. I began this project in the beginning of 2021 and I was interested in an application that addressed problems specific to the covid-19 pandemic.

Lean On Me serves two types of users: (a)users who are looking for aid and assistance opportunities and (b)users who are looking to help those impacted negatively from covid-19. Users will receive location based recommendations aligning with their needs.

Solution

I created 18 final mobile screens, larger device designs for 4 corresponding mobile screens, and a clickable prototype.

Project Retrospective

What Did You Learn?

This was a two part project. Part one focused on user experience methdologies while part two focused on user interface methdologies. I learned the value of interviewing people in the interaction design process. Interviews are important for understanding and uncovering user needs. I also learned for the first time how to design for larger screen sizes with responsive design. This was a challenging concept. However, I made sure to take my time with the responsive design section.

What Challenges Did You Encounter?

This project made me recognize and value users input when it comes to interaction design. Any step that involves real people needs to accomodate for additional time. Scheduling and recruiting the appropriate people for interviews and testing can be challenging.

What are the Next Steps?

This project I focused on sticking to my curated minimum viable product document. The next step would involve handing the design assets to engineers so that the application can be brought to life. Once the application is running, new features can be added and tested by users.
